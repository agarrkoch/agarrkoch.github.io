My name is Aida Garrido.
I am a playwright.

I like to use dialogue with violent efficiency, and to comic effect.

I have developed my work with The Tank, Romanian Cultural Institute, and The Drama League. I am a 2026 semi-finalist for Annie Baker's Cherry Lane Playwrights Collective.

I take my cues from Preston Sturges, Billy Wilder, and Ernst Lubitsch.

I believe that in deliberatly putting pen to paper, you will express something you have never been willing or able to articulate. Good writing is a moral act. It is the mind wrestling with itself, and the psyche struggling to set itself free. It is a process of revelation.

I am an archivist and audiovisual conservator.

I am passionate about open media and open standards. I never take for granted the legibility of digital objects and our access to them.

I am currently the Preservation Technician at CUNY TV.

I have also worked/consulted for WGBH, Dia Art Foundation, New York Public Radio, David Zwirner, Blank Forms, EYE Filmmuseum, Rietveld Academie, and Brooklyn Museum.

I have presented my work and held workshops at the No Time to Wait and Association of Moving Image Archivists conferences.

MA Film Preservation, University of Amsterdam. BA History, Princeton University.
